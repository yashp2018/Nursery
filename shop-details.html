About Grafted Tomato Sahil F1

The Sahil F1 is a premium hybrid tomato variety grafted onto wild Solanum torvum rootstock. This grafting technique provides natural immunity against soil-borne diseases that commonly affect tomato crops in Maharashtra.

Plant Characteristics
  • Plant Type: Indeterminate, vigorous growth
  • Fruit Type: Round, firm, deep red color
  • Fruit Weight: 80-100 grams
  • Maturity: 65-70 days after transplanting
  • Yield Potential: 50-60 tons per acre
Disease Resistance
  • Fusarium Wilt (Race 1, 2, 3)
  • Bacterial Wilt (Ralstonia)
  • Tomato Leaf Curl Virus
  • Root Knot Nematodes
Why Choose Grafted Plants?

Grafted plants combine the disease resistance of wild rootstocks with the high-yield characteristics of commercial varieties. This results in 30-50% higher productivity, reduced pesticide use, and better performance in problematic soils.

Complete Growing Guide
Climate Requirements

Temperature: 20-30°C optimal

Season: Kharif, Rabi & Summer (under shade)

Rainfall: Well-drained conditions preferred

Irrigation

Drip Irrigation: Recommended for best results

Frequency: Every 2-3 days in summer

Water Needs: 500-700mm per crop cycle

Soil Requirements

Soil Type: Well-drained loamy soil

pH Range: 6.0-7.0 optimal

Spacing: 60cm × 45cm (recommended)

Fertilizer Schedule

Basal Dose: 10 tons FYM + NPK 120:60:60 kg/acre

Top Dressing: 30 & 45 days after planting

Micronutrients: Boron & Calcium foliar sprays
